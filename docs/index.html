About Us

NodeSchool Karachi is a meetup dedicated to helping people learn JavaScript & with that, NodeJS. We help people of all skill levels–from beginners to experts–discover what's possible with JavaScript. Bring yourself, your computer, and the desire to learn to our next event!

NodeSchool events have been held around the world, and successfully introduced beginners to JavaScript, Node, and the command-line. Learn more about the international NodeSchool community at nodeschool.io

×

This is the official launch of the NodeSchool Karachi Chapter. The aim is to introduce NodeSchool, have a meetup to network with professionals. Teach & learn JavaScript and ultimately NodeJS. We'll share what our plans are going forward and will have some light talks as well as an `Angular` workshop too.

FAQs

What do I need to bring?
Bring a laptop, a desire to learn, and all your friendship!

Who is running the Karachi chapter?
Ahsan Ayaz ( @ahsanayaz) runs the Karachi chapter. Feel free to reach out for any questions.

What actually happens at a NodeSchool Karachi event?
NodeSchool Karachi events are an opportunity to hang out with other learners and mentors in a low stress, encouraging environment. Get set up to work on lessons with real industry tools on your own computer, at your own pace. A typical event follows this schedule:
  • Doors open
  • Introduciton & opening announcements (15 minutes)
  • Learning/mentoring (depending on event's timeline)
  • Refreshments (15-20 minutes)
  • Networking & Feedback
  • Event ends

What is a Focused Session?
Focused Sessions happen during our NodeSchool events. While our general event is free-form and self-directed, our Focused Sessions are more of a lecture-style format led by a mentor on special topics. Keep an eye on our website and Facebook page for announcements!

How much does it cost to attend?
NodeSchool Karachi is free! And open source!

How do I become a mentor?
We're always looking for people to volunteer to be mentors! We ask that prospective mentors:
  • Have already completed the core NodeSchool workshoppers before the event.
    • Having also completed some elective workshoppers is a great bonus.
  • Review the Mentor Best Practices wiki page.
  • Arrive 30 minutes early at the event so we can get setup and have our mentor meeting.
  • Sign up on the event's GitHub issue!

More questions?
File an issue at our GitHub repo and we'll answer as soon as possible.

Code Of Conduct

NodeSchool Karachi is dedicated to providing a harassment-free event experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion (or lack thereof), or technology choices. We do not tolerate harassment of event participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any event venue, including talks, workshops, parties, Twitter and other online media. Event participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the event at the discretion of the event organisers.
